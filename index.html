About Sindhi Culture

Sindhi culture has its roots in the civilization of the Indus Valley dating back to at least 2500 BCE. The culture flourished along the banks of the Indus River, developing a unique literary, musical and artistic tradition.

The Sindhi language was originally written in the Perso-Arabic script, with a rich literary tradition that includes the mystical poetry of Shah Abdul Latif Bhittai, Sachal Sarmast, and other Sufi saints. These works form a vital part of Sindhi cultural identity.

Traditional Sindhi music is characterized by instruments like the yaktaro (one-stringed lute), borindo (clay toy flute), and dholak (double-headed drum). The folk dances like Jhumar and Ho Jamalo showcase the energetic spirit of Sindhi culture.

The Ajrak, a block-printed shawl with intricate geometric designs in deep indigo and crimson, is one of the most recognizable symbols of Sindhi culture, representing the region's artisanal heritage.
